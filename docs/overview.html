Event Ledger

A small system that records money moving between accounts — safely, even when things go wrong.

What is this?

Two small services that work together to record financial events — like "deposit $150" or "withdraw $40" — for thousands of accounts. One service is the front door for clients; the other keeps track of the actual balances. Both run side-by-side in Docker.

Why does it matter?

Money systems can't make two kinds of mistakes: they can't charge you twice for the same thing, and they can't lose your transaction when something breaks. This project is built around making both of those mistakes impossible — and being honest when something is broken.

▣ How it's wired

Two services, two databases, one synchronous HTTP call between them. Nothing fancy on the wire — all the cleverness is in how they handle failure.

★ What makes it interesting

Same event twice? No problem.

If the network hiccups and your phone sends the same payment twice, the system records it once. Always.

Events out of order? Sorted.

Upstream banks don't deliver events in order. The system still shows them in the right sequence and computes the right balance.

Half the system is down? Still useful.

If the balance service is down, the gateway still accepts your transaction, stores it safely, and applies it the moment things recover.

Always knows what happened.

Every state change is recorded in an audit log. Every log line carries a "trace ID" so one customer request can be followed across both services.
